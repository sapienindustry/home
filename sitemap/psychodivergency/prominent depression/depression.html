About Major Depression

Depression is a state of persistent sadness and loss of interest (even in things that usually bring pleasure). When a person experiences Major Depression, they are not able to continue with day to day activites owing to the fatigue that accompanies a depressed mood.

At the same time people are likely to reinforce and maintain behaviors that encourage lethargy and decreased ambition like eating less, inconsistent sleeping and rehearsing negative cognitions of themselves and existence.

Etiology of Major Depression

Why do people experience Major Depression?

  • Neuropsychological perspectives: suggest that a balance in neurochemicals (dopamine, serotonin and norepinephrine) plays a role in mood and volition. The balance is informed by sleep patterns (and other biological rhythms) which can impact a persons mood. When these chemicals are out of balance then a person may be vulnerable to Major Depression and other threats to mental-health.

  • Cognitive perspectives: suggest that it is the negative aprraisals of a situation that prevent positive behaviors. This appraisal prevents behavior that would've ammended the situation that needed to change. The repetition of negative appraisals form a bias that prevent positive behaviors that benefit the pursuit of one's ambitions.
