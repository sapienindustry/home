About Bipolar Mood

Typically, the experience of Bipolar Mood is described by periods that fluctuate between intense exhilaration and periods of significant depression. When people do not know that they are experiencing Bipolar Mood, they are at risk of severe mood swings, impulsive thoughts and behaviors that impede on positive mental health.

Depending on the severity of these experiences, it often occurs that people with Bipolar Mood experience changes in sleep patterns, productivity, and are more likely to take part in risky and impulsive behaviours (unwarranted purchases, drunk driving etc).

Did you know: Many famous artists, musicians, writers, and politicians appear to have had Bipolar Moods as it is believed to be associated with elevated creativity and productivity.

Etiology of Bipolar Mood

Why do people experience Bipolar?

There are many perspectives that discuss the etiology of Bipolar Mood, yet it is not fully understood. The main perspectives are:

  • Developmental perspectives: which suggest that there is an interplay between the environmental (nurtured) and hereditary (natural) elements that a person experiences which inspire Bipolar Moods.

  • Neuropsychological perspectives: which suggest that neurotransmitters such as serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine play a role in the experience of Bipolar Mood. These factors are informed by sleep patterns (and other biological rhythms) which can impact a persons mood.

    • Ways to improve mental health while experiencing Bipolar Mood.

    The best way to maintain positive mental health is by ensuring you are sleeping in a routine and considerate of the things you consume (media and food). This is because the neurochemistry of the mind depends on sleep, good health and positivity to excel.
