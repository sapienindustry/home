ABOUT

A sound recall task allows a person to measure their ability to recall sounds (echoic memory).


TASK SPECIFICATION:

What the task measures.

  • The average proximity of the sounds (in Hertz) you recalled and the original sounds (recall accuracy).
  • The average amount of time it took (in milliseconds) to recall the sounds (response latency).

After you complete the task you will be shown a graphical representation of each specification

The Sound Recall Task should take about 12 minutes to complete.

PREQUISITES:

To get the best out of this study:

  • Ensure you are seated in a quite environment.
  • Ensure the volume on your device is turned all the way up.
  • If you are wearing hearing aids, it is recommended that you remove them.
  • The page will play a sound when the timer is over so you may continue with other work in the meantime.

    • This task should not be used for diagnostic purposes. It is only intended to provide you with a basic description of echoic memory.

    If you think you are experiencing problems with your echoic memory, please consult a neuropsychologist.
